[Vol. 1, No. 1] Reversible Photodegradation of Fluorescent Markers on Unread Lab Notebooks  -  L. Biochemist & Y. Archivist  (2026)
Abstract (EN).
We investigate the long-term stability of fluorescent markers applied to biochemical lab notebooks that are never opened again after the experiment. By exposing the closed notebooks to controlled daylight cycles, we show that photodegradation of the ink proceeds independently of scientific impact. Our results confirm that, even in complete academic obscurity, entropy proceeds at a respectable pace.

[Vol. 1, No. 1] Closed-Circuit Wastewater Recycling for Infinite Lab Dishwashing  -  Q. Environmentalist & Z. Technician  (2026)
Abstract (EN).
This work designs a closed-circuit wastewater system dedicated to the perpetual washing of glassware used in low-impact environmental experiments. Using a combination of over-engineered filters and under-documented valves, we achieve infinite dishwashing cycles with no visible improvement in experimental outcomes. The system successfully demonstrates how resource conservation can coexist with complete scientific irrelevance.
摘要 (ZH)。
本研究搭建了一套专门用于“低影响环境实验玻璃器皿”无限循环清洗的封闭式废水系统。 通过过度复杂的滤芯与说明书缺失的阀门组合，我们实现了看似永动的刷试管流程， 却没有在任何实验结果上观察到可量化的提升。该系统成功示范了：在科研几乎毫无意义的前提下， 资源节约依然可以被过度设计到极致。
[Vol. 1, No. 1] Finite-Element Simulation of Cracking in Decorative Concrete That Nobody Walks On  -  M. Materials & R. Modeler  (2026)
Abstract (EN).
We present a high-resolution finite-element model of crack initiation and propagation in decorative concrete panels installed in a restricted rooftop garden with no public access. The simulations accurately reproduce the micro-cracking patterns observed under rare maintenance visits while having zero implications for structural safety. Our findings suggest that numerical methods can be exquisitely precise even when the underlying problem does not matter at all.
摘要 (ZH)。
